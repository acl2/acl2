Here are just a few of the opportunities for future work using
ACL2(zfc).
-- Matt Kaufmann, list begun on July 4, 2026

----------

Provide more automation, in particular to improve (and possibly
rename) the macro defthme defined in utilities/defthme.lisp to provide
more help with reasoning involving quantifiers.

----------

Introduce definitions and lemmas for basic set theory notions, e.g.:

- cardinals and cardinality;
- Cartesian product;
- ultrafilter;
- equivalence relation; and
- ultraproduct.

----------

Implement transfinite induction and recursion (some progress by Matt
K., not yet in the library as of 6/2026).

----------

Extend topology/.

----------

Extend algebra/.  Perhaps consider David Russinoff's work under
books/projects/ in groups/ and fields/.

----------

Reals (barely started) and then continuous math

----------

"Interdisciplinary" opportunities include:

- metric spaces (involving reals and topology), and more generally,
  calculus and analysis;

- polynomial rings over the reals; and

- the complex numbers via ordered pairs of reals, forming an
  algebraically closed field (also, consider subfields).

----------

Develop temporal logics without working around the lack of infinite
paths in ACL2.

----------

Pursue higher-order logic, e.g.:

- Continue work on translating HOL to ACL2(zfc) (joint work with
  Konrad Slind).

- Formalize HOL proof theorem and semantics and prove soundness.

----------

Pursue more advanced set theory topics (some of which have been
pursued already with other proof assistants).  Here are a few
example areas to consider.

- Ordinal and cardinal arithmetic

- Closed unbounded and stationary subsets of omega_1

- Mostowski collapse lemma

- Independence results

- Large cardinals

- Infinitary combinatorics, starting with Ramsey's theorem

----------

Develop first-order logic syntax and semantics.  Then, for example:

- Prove soundness and completeness theorems.

- Consider models of set theory; perhaps figure out how to formalize
  and prove the Reflection Theorem.

- Develop some model theory.

----------
